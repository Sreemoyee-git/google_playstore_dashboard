# google_playstore_dashboard

Problem Statement:

The task is to analyze the Google Playstore data to identify trends, patterns, and insights that can inform app development, marketing strategies, and user experience improvements. The analysis focuses on aspects such as app ratings, install counts, app types, and categories, which can help app developers understand how their apps perform in the market and what types of apps are gaining traction.

What I Did:

Cleaned and structured the data for analysis, ensuring that values like ratings, install counts, app categories, and content ratings were properly formatted and free from inconsistencies.

Used visualizations like bar charts, pie charts, and line graphs to explore trends in app ratings by type, install patterns by genre, and category distribution.

Built an interactive dashboard that shows key metrics such as:

Average ratings and size of apps.
App types distribution (free vs paid).
Install counts by genre and app categories.
Content ratings distribution.
App count by year, helping to analyze app growth over time.

Tools Used:

Power BI: Used for creating the interactive dashboard and performing visual data analysis.

Excel: Used for initial data cleaning, transformation, and preparation before importing it into Power BI.

Visualizations: Leveraged Power BI's built-in charts (bar, pie, line charts) to generate insightful visualizations.

Recommendations:

1. Focus on Popular Genres:

The analysis shows that genres like Action, Casual, and Adventure dominate in terms of installs. Developers should focus on these genres for their next projects to tap into the largest user base.

2. Optimize App Size:

The average app size is 22.75 MB. For better user retention, developers can optimize the app size to ensure it remains below this average, as larger apps may deter users with limited storage.

3. Increase Free App Offerings:

A significant portion of apps are paid. However, free apps tend to have more installs and a higher count of users. Offering free trials or freemium models could increase app engagement.

4. Target Family and Game Categories:

Apps in the Game and Family categories have the most installs. Developers could consider targeting these categories with engaging content, such as educational games or family-friendly apps.

Conclusion:

Through this analysis of the Google Playstore data, several key insights have been uncovered:

Action and Casual genres are the most successful, and apps within these categories perform better in terms of installs.

There is a clear correlation between free apps and higher install counts, suggesting that freemium models may be more effective in attracting users.

North America leads in total installs, with family-friendly apps and games being the dominant categories in the market.

The insights gained from the dashboard can help app developers prioritize their efforts in popular genres, optimize app sizes, and better understand the importance of free app offerings to capture more users. Future analysis could further refine recommendations by exploring user ratings trends over time or comparing regional performance for deeper market insights.
